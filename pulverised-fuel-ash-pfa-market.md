<p><p>Pulverised Fuel Ash (PFA) is a byproduct of coal combustion in power plants, classified primarily into Class C and Class F. Class C PFA contains high calcium, suitable for applications in cement and concrete production, offering benefits in strength and durability. Class F PFA has low calcium content and is derived from anthracite or bituminous coal, serving as a pozzolanic material, enhancing concrete's long-term strength and reducing permeability. Both classes serve distinct roles in construction and civil engineering.</p></p>
